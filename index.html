biography

1. Early Life:
Born on April 27, 1936, in Bhopal, India, Abdul Qadeer Khan moved to Pakistan after its independence in 1947. He showed early promise in science and engineering.

2. Education:
Khan pursued his education in Germany and Belgium, earning degrees in metallurgical engineering and later a doctorate in engineering metallurgy.

3. Career Beginnings:
In the early 1970s, Khan joined the Netherlands' URENCO Group, where he gained expertise in uranium enrichment technologies.

4. Nuclear Espionage Allegations:
Khan faced controversy for his involvement in the illicit transfer of nuclear technology to Iran, North Korea, and Libya in the 1980s and 1990s. This tarnished his reputation internationally but elevated him to a hero status in Pakistan.

5. Father of Pakistan's Nuclear Bomb:
Khan's pivotal role in Pakistan's nuclear weapons program earned him the title of the "Father of the Pakistani Bomb." He was instrumental in the development of Pakistan's nuclear arsenal, defying international sanctions and pressures.
